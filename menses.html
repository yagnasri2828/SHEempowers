Supplementaries

Fruits and Vegetables: Offer a variety of fruits and vegetables to ensure they get a range of vitamins and minerals. Aim for different colors to diversify nutrient intake.
Proteins: Include sources such as lean meats (cut into small, manageable pieces), fish, eggs, and legumes like beans and lentils.
Dairy: Offer whole milk (unless advised otherwise by a healthcare provider), cheese, and yogurt to support calcium needs for bone development.
Grains: Choose whole grains like whole wheat bread, brown rice, oats, and cereals to provide fiber and energy.
Healthy Fats: Include sources like avocado, nut butters (if no allergies are present), and small amounts of vegetable oils.

Handwashing: Teach toddlers to wash hands thoroughly before eating and after using the bathroom.
Oral Hygiene: Start brushing with a soft-bristled toothbrush and water (no fluoride toothpaste needed at this age unless recommended by a dentist).
Safe Food Handling: Ensure food is prepared and stored safely to prevent contamination.
