Supplementaries

Fruits and Vegetables:Aim for a variety of colors and types. Fresh, frozen, or canned without added sugars are all good options. Examples include apples, bananas, carrots, and spinach.
Proteins: Include lean meats, poultry, fish, eggs, nuts, seeds, and legumes.
Dairy: Provide milk, cheese, yogurt, and other dairy products, preferably low-fat or fat-free.
Grains: Whole grains like brown rice, oatmeal, whole wheat bread, and pasta are preferable.
Healthy Fats: Include sources like avocados, nuts, seeds, and olive oil.

Handwashing: Teach toddlers to wash hands thoroughly before eating and after using the bathroom.
Dental Care: Brush teeth twice daily with fluoride toothpaste and encourage regular dental check-ups.
Bathing: Regular baths or showers to keep the skin clean.
Hair Care: Regular washing and brushing of hair.
Nail Care: Keep nails trimmed and clean.
Toilet Hygiene: Teach proper wiping techniques and the importance of flushing and washing hands afterward.

Vitamin D: Depending on sunlight exposure and dietary intake, a supplement may be recommended by a healthcare provider.
Iron: Toddlers who do not consume enough iron-rich foods (like meats and fortified cereals) may need a supplement, but this should be guided by a healthcare provider's advice.
Multivitamins: If recommended by a pediatrician, a daily multivitamin designed for children can help fill nutritional gaps.
Probiotics: Can be beneficial for digestive health, available in certain yogurts or as supplements.
Omega-3 Fatty Acids: Important for brain development, available in certain fish or as supplements.
